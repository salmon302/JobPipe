1. Architectural Guardrails (Strict Formatting)To ensure the resulting PDF can be converted into a clean stream of plain text by basic parsing engines, you must adhere to the following structural constraints:
Mandate Single-Column Layout: You must use a strict single-column layout. Do not use minipage, twocolumn, or complex grid environments, as parsers read left to right and will scramble interleaved text.  
Prohibit Complex Elements: Completely avoid using tables, text boxes, and any embedded graphics. Do not include logos, charts, infographics, or visual skill-rating progress bars.  
Main Body Placement Only: Place all critical information, especially contact details (name, phone, email, LinkedIn), within the main body of the document. Never use LaTeX \header or \footer environments for this data, as parsers frequently skip them.  
Standard Typography & Symbols: Use standard professional fonts (e.g., Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, Georgia) and avoid custom or decorative fonts. Avoid underlining entirely, as the line can intersect with descending letters and cause character recognition errors.  
Bullet Points and Characters: Use only standard, solid round (•) or square (▪) bullet points. Strictly avoid custom symbols like arrows or checkmarks. Strip out all special characters and accents, replacing words like "résumé" with "resume".  
2. Structural Signposts & CategorizationATS parsers rely on predictable structures to segment data into database fields.  Standardized Section Headings: You must use conventional headings: "Work Experience," "Education," "Skills," "Certifications," and "Professional Summary". Never generate creative alternatives like "My Professional Journey" or "My Toolkit".  Chronological Order: Organize all experience in a reverse-chronological format, starting with the most recent position.  Consistent Date Formatting: Format all employment and education dates uniformly as MM/YYYY or Month YYYY. Always include both the month and the year to ensure the ATS can accurately calculate the candidate's total years of experience.  
3. Content Engineering & Keyword StrategyThe resume must satisfy both basic keyword filters and advanced semantic AI models.  Exact Keyword Matching: Extract required skills, software, and terminology from the target job description and mirror them in the resume using exact phrasing.  The Acronym Rule: For every technical term, certification, or methodology, include both the full term and the acronym in parentheses on the first use (e.g., "Project Management Professional (PMP)").  Job Title Translation: If a user provides a creative or non-standard past job title (e.g., "Growth Guru"), map it to the industry-standard equivalent that aligns with the target role (e.g., "Business Development Manager").  Contextual Integration: Do not keyword stuff. Weave keywords naturally into the contextual fabric of achievement-oriented bullet points. A keyword tied to a quantified achievement is weighted far more heavily by advanced systems than a keyword isolated in a list.  
4. Quantification and ImpactAdvanced systems and human recruiters rank candidates based on evidence of impact.  PAR/STAR Framework: Restructure all responsibility-focused bullet points into the PAR (Problem-Action-Result) or STAR (Situation-Task-Action-Result) format.  Mandatory Metrics: Actively prompt for or extract quantifiable metrics across four key domains: Money (revenue, budgets), Time (efficiency, deadlines), Volume/Scale (team size, client load), and Percentages (growth, improvement).  Credible Estimates: If exact numbers are not provided, apply well-reasoned estimates using qualifiers such as "approximately," "over," or "nearly," as a reasoned estimate is far more impactful than omitting a metric.
